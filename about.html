About Me

I am a refugee rights, sexual reproductive health and rights (SRHR) advocate, refugee researcher and a peace builder from South Sudan living in Uganda as a refugee. My advocacy, research and peace building experience in displaced settings spans to five years.

Having experienced displacement myself, my passion in advocating for my fellow refugee rights stems from my own experiences of displacement. I am currently a co-founder and program coordinator at Youth Action for Community Transformation (YACOT) and served with many international organizations like Windle International Uganda, Resettlement Support Center (RSC Africa), IDinsight, Center for Evaluation and Development Uganda (C4ED) and Give Directly in refugee camps from Uganda.

As the co-founder and program coordinator of Youth Action for Community Transformation (YACOT), I have developed and implemented advocacy programs in areas of sexual reproductive health and rights and peace building. These advocacy programs include dialogues, trainings and awareness campaigns which aimed at raising awareness on refugee rights especially in areas of sexual reproductive health and rights and peace building. The programs I have developed and implemented directly impacted 3000 refugee and host community members from Rhino Camp Refugee Settlement.

In addition, I am a certified peace builder trained by the African Centre for the Constructive Resolution of Disputes (ACCORD) and International Center for Peace, Human Rights and Development (IPHRD-Africa) on conflict management and resolution, mediation and dialogue. I am also an alumni of Kuza Amani and Youth in Action Fellowships. I also was selected to represent refugee youth by the United Nation Human Rights Office of the High Commissioner for East and Horn of Africa in a consultation meeting on fostering youth participation in governance, peace and security in the context of human rights 75 in 2023.
