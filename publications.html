  • The Role of Refugee Youth in Peace Building: A Case Study of Rhino Camp Refugee Settlement
    Uganda hosts one of the largest refugee populations in Africa, with significant numbers of youth residing in settlements like Rhino Camp in the West Nile sub-region (KRC Uganda, 2025). These young people, having often experienced conflict and displacement, represent both a vulnerable demographic and a significant, largely untapped resource for peacebuilding. While exact figures fluctuate due to new arrivals and verification exercises, as of late 2024 and early 2025, UNHCR data and related reports indicate Uganda hosts over 1.7 million refugees and asylum seekers in total. The international community has increasingly recognized the crucial role of young people in maintaining and promoting peace and security through frameworks like UN Security Council Resolution 2250 (2015) and subsequent resolutions (2419 and 2535), which collectively form the Youth, Peace and Security (YPS) Agenda. This agenda calls for the meaningful participation of youth in all phases of peace processes, from conflict prevention and resolution to post-conflict reconstruction and reconciliation.

  • Community Organization Advances SRHR for Adolescents in Rhino Camp Refugee Settlement
    Community transformation centers the work and leadership of Youth Action for Community Transformation (YACOT), a community-based organization advancing sexual and reproductive health and rights (SRHR) in northwest Uganda. YACOT is based in the Odobu Zone of Rhino Camp Refugee Settlement, serving refugees (mostly from neighboring South Sudan) and members of the host community.
